Welcome to Save Our Earth

We have a duty to leave the Earth better than how we found it. As humans, we must do our best to care for our home. This is a moral obligation. Learn about climate change, its causes, and what you can do to help preserve our planet.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While natural processes can cause climate fluctuations, current rapid changes are primarily driven by human activities. Learn more at NASA Climate Change.

Why Climate Change Matters

Climate change affects ecosystems, human health, and economies worldwide. Rising temperatures lead to more frequent extreme weather events, loss of biodiversity, and threats to food and water security. The IPCC Sixth Assessment Report provides detailed findings.

Key Greenhouse Gases

  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂): Produced by burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industry.
  • Methane (CH₄): Emitted from agriculture, landfills, and fossil fuel extraction.
  • Nitrous oxide (N₂O): Released from fertilizers and industrial processes.
  • Fluorinated gases: Synthetic gases used in refrigerants and industry.

Global Warming Trends

Earth’s average surface temperature has risen about 1.2°C since 1880. Ice sheets are shrinking, sea levels are rising, and extreme heat events are increasing. Data from NASA Global Temperature show the trends.

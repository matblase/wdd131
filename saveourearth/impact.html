The Impact of Climate Change

Explore how climate change affects ecosystems, human health, and economies worldwide.

Environmental Effects

Rising temperatures cause glaciers to melt, sea levels to rise, and extreme weather events to become more frequent. Coral reefs are bleaching, and habitats for wildlife are shrinking. Learn more at National Geographic.

Effects on Ecosystems

  • Species migration or extinction due to habitat loss
  • Changes in plant flowering and animal breeding cycles
  • Ocean acidification impacting marine life

Human Health Risks

  • Increased heat-related illnesses and deaths
  • Spread of infectious diseases (e.g., malaria)
  • Food and water shortages affecting nutrition

Economic Consequences

Extreme weather, flooding, and droughts cause billions in damages annually. Agriculture, fisheries, and tourism are particularly affected. More details at UN Climate Change.

Global Initiatives & Policies

International agreements like the Paris Agreement aim to limit global warming to well below 2Â°C. Governments, NGOs, and communities worldwide are implementing renewable energy, reforestation, and sustainable practices.

How You Can Help

Everyone can make a difference: reduce energy use, recycle, plant trees, and advocate for climate policies. Learn actionable steps at WWF.
